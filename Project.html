Immersive Insights Into Nature: Environmental Monitoring Dashboards for Kawartha Conservation

StoryMap

One of the final deliverables for this project consist of a StoryMap which will be utilized by Kawartha Conservation on their public facing website to allow the public to interact and engage with the data collected by the selected Monitoring Programs in a meaningful and impactful way. The StoryMap provides the user with both background information and an understanding of the programs and their importance to watershed management. Embedded within the StoryMap are three interactive Esri Dashboards which feature data Kawartha Conservation has collected related to these programs which, along with the background information provides the public with a visual aid to understand and interpret the data being presented.

Biomonitoring Dashboard

The Biomonitoring Dashboard was created to show the trends of Family Biotic Index and Sensitive Organisms for monitoring sites, using a dataset with a date range from 2005 to 2023. The following figures show the Dashboard when there is no site selected and again with a site selected. The scatter plots connected with lines show the trends of each monitoring site over the years. Each site is classified into different colors, as shown in the legend on the right. The graphs are also shaded with different colors for different categories. In addition, when hovering over the points in the graphs, the Dashboard shows the date when the site was inspected. The white circle at the top of the graph enables users to change the time frame by moving it to the left or right. When a site is selected, the site details and a picture of the site are shown. The user can also filter the site by selecting different habitat type, site type and year using the selectors at the top. There's also a collapsible sidebar on the left, where the user can click on it to find the dashboard instructions.

Water Temperature Dashboard

The Water Temperature Dashboard was created to show data collected monthly at sample sites across the Kawartha Watershed and the percent of each month where the recorded water temperature was less the 22 °C which is the favorable water temperature for Brook Trout habitat. The user is prompted to select the site by clicking on the site selector in the upper right corner of the Dashboard when there is no selection made. Once a site is selected a series of interactive data is created. The map zooms to the corresponding site location, the Brook Trout Habitat Preference graph is populated showing the percentage of days where the water temperature was under 22 °C each month, which is preferable for Brook Trout habitat. After a site selection is made the Water Temperature Recordings graph also populates showing the minimum, average and maximum temperatures recorded each month for the corresponding Site location. Additionally, the Water Temperature and Habitat Preference Table is populated summarizing the data presented in both graphs for the given site presenting the data in a usable manner for further exploration.

Water Quality Dashboard

The Water Quality Dashboard was created to show trends in the Provincial Water Quality Monitoring Network data, using a sample dataset that spans 5 years. Each section of the Dashboard prompts the user to click on the dropdown menus on the top right to select a category and year. This bar graph shows the percentage of samples in a station that have passed/failed a given threshold for the selected category and year. A picture on the top left shows a photograph of each selected site. Since the photographs were not provided at the time of Dashboard completion, a placeholder image has been added to this section. This image can be replaced within the “Inputs” section of the PWQMN script.
